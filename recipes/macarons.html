Macarons

These colorful bite-sized confections are just the treat. They have a smooth and crisp shell filled with buttercream or jam. The sky's the limit with flavors and fillings for these adorably sweet concoctions!

Ingredients

For The Cookie

  • 3 large egg whites
  • 1 ½ almond flour
  • ½ granulated sugar
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp. vanilla
  • ¼ tsp. of tartar

Filling

  • 1 cup unsalted butter softened
  • 5 egg yolks
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp. vanilla
  • 3 Tbsp. water
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

For the Wafers

  1. In a medium sized bowl sift together almond flour and confectioners sugar.
  2. Add room temperature egg whites to a clean bowl.
  3. Whisk egg whites until they begin to foam. Then add the cream of tartar and slowly add the granulated sugar.
  4. Add food coloring (if desired) and vanilla then mix in. Continue to beat until stiff peaks form.
  5. Fold in ⅓ of the dry ingredients
    6. .
  6. Gently fold in the rest of the ingredients.
  7. Carefully spoon mixture into a piping bag with round piping tip.
  8. Pipe one inch dollops onto lined baking sheet. Tap onto counter to remove air bubbles.
  9. Allow to sit for 40 minutes before placing in the oven.
  10. Bake at 3000F. for 12 to 15 minutes, rotate tray after 7 minutes.
  11. Allow to cool completely before removing from baking sheet.

Filling Instructions

  1. Combine sugar and water in medium saucepan. Heat over low heat while stirring until sugar completely dissolves.
  2. Mix egg yolks in a bowl until thick and foamy.
  3. Cook the sugar and water syrup until it reaches 2400F. Immediately remove from heat.
  4. Slowly incorporate the sugar mixture to the eggs while mixing. Continue mixing until the bottom of the bowl has completely cooled.
  5. Add in butter one cube at a time. Add vanilla and salt. Continue mixing until the buttercream mixture is smooth and creamy.

Assembly Instructions

  1. Pipe the filling onto the back of a cookie shell and form sandwhiches.
  2. For best results let Macarons rest in the frigefor one to three days.
